  • PF

  • What is PF?

    Provident Fund (PF) is a government-backed retirement savings scheme aimed at providing financial security to employees upon retirement.

  • Who contributes to PF?

    Both the employer and the employee make contributions to the PF account.

  • How is PF calculated?

    PF contributions are usually a fixed percentage of the employee's salary, typically 12% of the basic salary.

  • ESI

  • What is ESI?

    Employee State Insurance (ESI) is a self-financing social security and health insurance scheme for Indian workers.

  • Who is covered under ESI?

    ESI applies to employees earning a salary of up to Rs. 21,000 per month working in specified sectors covered under the ESI Act.

  • What are the benefits of ESI?

    ESI provides medical benefits, sickness benefits, maternity benefits, disablement benefits, and dependent benefits to covered employees and their families.

  • Can I avail medical benefits under ESI for my family members?

    Yes, dependent family members of covered employees are also eligible for medical benefits under ESI.

  • PT

  • What is Profession Tax?

    Profession Tax is a state-level tax levied on the income earned by individuals engaged in various professions, trades, and employments.

  • Who is liable to pay Profession Tax?

    Individuals earning an income from salary, profession, trade, or employment are liable to pay Profession Tax.

  • How is Profession Tax calculated?

    Profession Tax rates vary from state to state and are usually based on the individual's income slab.
